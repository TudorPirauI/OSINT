## Solution

The first step to identifying the spot where the original picture was taken was to perform a reverse image search. Using Google Images returned several visual matches of the building shown in the photo. I confirmed it was the same building because it featured the initials `KBS`, matching the structure in the provided image.

<img width="975" height="518" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1ac2c3d9-acdc-4cc6-ac10-4e7cd9309b9b" />


Next, I visited a webpage from the search results that featured a similar image, specifically `worldradiomap.com`.

<img width="975" height="518" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0dfef290-01c5-4fb3-ba34-f36210127e06" />


I initially couldn't find the exact image from the Google search on the page, so I performed a more extensive search within the site.

<img width="975" height="231" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5a878f42-908b-4000-af2a-56feadf580cf" />


Scrolling to the bottom of the page, I located specific details about the photo's origin and the `KBS` building's location. This revealed that the building is situated on `Wopsononock Lookout` mountain, located northwest of Altoona, Pennsylvania.

<img width="975" height="518" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d3c0e3e2-fa49-4a39-947e-2248bf5e256c" />


Finally, I searched for the mountain's specific name and found a dedicated historical website. According to this site, locals refer to the mountain as `Wopsy`.

**The flag is:** `DawgCTF{Wopsy}`
